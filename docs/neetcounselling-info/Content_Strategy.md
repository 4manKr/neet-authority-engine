# Content Strategy for neetcounselling.info

## 1. Executive Summary
The primary goal of `neetcounselling.info` is to establish authority in the highly competitive NEET medical admissions space in India. By providing accurate, timely, and hyper-organized information, the site aims to attract organic traffic from 24+ lakh annual aspirants and convert high-intent users into leads for premium counselling services (like TAB India).

## 2. Target Audience & Search Intent
Our content must address the specific anxieties and informational gaps of different user segments:
*   **The High Scorer (600+):** Looking for AIIMS/top government college cut-offs, MCC choice-filling tricks, and AIQ (All India Quota) vs. State Quota strategies.
*   **The Borderline Scorer (450 - 590):** Looking for low-fee private medical colleges, state-specific semi-government seats, and alternative options (BDS/AYUSH).
*   **The Low Scorer (< 450):** Looking for Management quota seats, NRI quota, Deemed Universities fee structures, and MBBS Abroad options.
*   **The Parents:** Looking for fee structures, hidden charges, safety, and step-by-step guidance on the complex documentation process.

## 3. Core Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: The "Ultimate Guides" (Evergreen Content)
These are comprehensive, long-form guides designed to rank for broad keywords and establish domain authority.
*   **The Complete Guide to MCC NEET UG Counselling 2026**
*   **State-by-State NEET Counselling Hub:** A central directory linking to dedicated pages for every single state (e.g., *UP NEET Counselling Process*, *Karnataka KEA Medical Admissions*).
*   **Understanding Quotas:** AIQ (15%) vs. State Quota (85%) explained simply.
*   **Document Checklist:** The ultimate list of documents required for medical counselling in India.

### Pillar 2: Data-Driven Content (High Link-Building Potential)
Aspirants are desperate for historical data. Formatting this into clean, searchable tables is a massive traffic driver.
*   **Previous Year Cut-offs:** State-wise and category-wise closing ranks for Government & Private colleges.
*   **Fee Structures:** Comprehensive databases of Private College and Deemed University fee structures (including hostel & hidden fees).
*   **Seat Matrix:** Expected seat matrix for the current year.

### Pillar 3: Tool-Based Content
If possible to develop, these pages have the highest engagement and retention.
*   NEET College Predictor (based on previous year data).
*   Rank vs. Marks historical analysis.

## 4. Seasonal Content Calendar (The "Launch" Strategy)

Search volume for NEET is highly seasonal. The content calendar must align with the exam lifecycle.

### Phase 1: Pre-Exam (Jan - April)
*   **Focus:** Preparation tips, exam pattern, syllabus, expected cut-offs, and building initial domain trust.
*   **Key Articles:** "Expected NEET 2026 Cut off", "How to calculate NEET score", "Top 10 Medical Colleges in India".

### Phase 2: Post-Exam & Pre-Results (May - June)
*   *Traffic starts spiking.*
*   **Focus:** Answer keys, paper analysis, Marks vs. Rank predictions.
*   **Key Articles:** "NEET 2026 Answer Key Solutions", "What rank can I expect for 550 marks?", "Top Private Colleges for Low Scores".

### Phase 3: The Counselling Rush (July - October)
*   *Peak Traffic Season.*
*   **Focus:** Breaking news, schedule updates, choice-filling strategies, and mock allotments.
*   **Key Articles:** "MCC Round 1 Schedule Released", "Step-by-step Choice Filling Guide", "State Merit List PDFs (Live Updates)".

## 5. SEO & On-Page Guidelines
*   **URL Structure:** Keep URLs clean and hierarchical (e.g., `neetcounselling.info/state/uttar-pradesh/cut-off`).
*   **Dynamic Updating:** Append the current year "2026" to titles and meta tags, and update them annually to maintain relevance.
*   **Table of Contents:** Use sticky Table of Contents plugins/components for long articles to improve user experience and generate jump-links in Google SERPs.
*   **Schema Markup:** Implement `FAQPage` schema on every major guide, and `Article` schema for news updates.

## 6. Conversion Strategy (Monetization / Lead Gen)
Traffic without conversion is useless. Integrate the following strategies to capture leads:
*   **Gated Content:** "Download the full PDF of 2025 State-wise Cut-offs" (Requires Name, Phone Number, NEET Score).
*   **Sticky CTAs:** A persistent footer or sidebar CTA: *"Confused about choice filling? Talk to our Expert Counsellors today."*
*   **In-Content Forms:** After a section discussing low scores, insert a form: *"Find out which private colleges you can get with your score. [Check Now]"*
*   **WhatsApp Community:** Add a button to join a dedicated WhatsApp/Telegram group for live updates (excellent for building a retargetable audience).
